One Preserve. One Vision.

More nature than you expected, closer than you think.

The Palos Preserves stretch across southwest Cook County — a mosaic of hilly woods, peat bogs, glacial sloughs, and wetlands that together form the largest uninterrupted forested landscape in all of Cook County. It is, in every meaningful sense, a wilderness at your doorstep.

World Record

The night sky Chicago forgot it had.

In 2021, the Palos Preserves became the largest Urban Night Sky Place on Earth, certified by the International Dark-Sky Association in partnership with the Adler Planetarium. Satellite data confirms what visitors already feel: this forest is astonishingly, measurably dark.

Teens from Adler's Far Horizons program used ISS data to document that Palos emits nearly 1,000 times less artificial light than downtown Chicago. Star parties, nighttime trail walks, and the Camp Bullfrog Lake campground put you under skies that few Chicagoans know exist.

Where to Sleep

Stay in
the woods.

Camp Bullfrog Lake is the only overnight option inside the preserves — and it's a remarkable one. Nestled at the heart of the trail system, it's the ideal base camp for a Palos weekend. Reservations available online through the Forest Preserves of Cook County.

Tent & RV Campsites
Primitive tent sites and electric RV hookups (30/50 amp) right at the water's edge. Free bundle of oak firewood with every reservation. Up to 6 people per site.
From $21/night (tent) · $27/night (RV electric)
Cabins (Heated & Standard)
Small cabins (up to 8 people) and large cabins (up to 10), some winterized with private bathrooms. Perfect for family weekends or small group retreats.
From $42/night · Various configurations
Bunkhouses & Group Sites
Small bunkhouses (16 people) and large bunkhouses for youth groups, teams, and non-profit organizations. Veterans and non-profits receive 50% discounts Sun–Thu.
Group pricing available · 50% discount for nonprofits & veterans

Plan Your Visit

Everything you need to get out here.

Palos Preserves is free to visit and open year-round. Most trailheads have free parking and are accessible from multiple surrounding communities. The preserves close at sunset — except for registered campers at Camp Bullfrog Lake and special events.

Visit the Official Preserves Site →
Hours
Open sunrise to sunset daily. Registered campers have 24-hour access. Staff on-site 8am–4pm at Camp Bullfrog Lake.
Getting Here
~30 min southwest of Chicago. 43 parking locations throughout the system. Limited Metra access via Heritage Line. No admission fee.
Camping Reservations
Book at fpdcc.com or call the campground. Must be 18+ to reserve, 21+ for cabins. Reservations less than 48 hours out must be by phone.
Trail Conditions
Check Trailforks.com or CAMBR.org before riding. Wet trails close to bikes — if you leave a footprint, turn around. Orange markers = closed trail.
